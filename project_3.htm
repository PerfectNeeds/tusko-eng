Solvay Alexandria

Design supply and installation.
Solvay factory in Alexandria, was built in the sixties by the Romanians, as part of the Soviet block aid program to President Nasser. The factory is on eof the main producers of Soda Ash in Egypt. Storage bins are used to store Soda ash, which are used in a multitude of industries including beverages, and detergents. Soda ash powder is dust generating, and a health hazard. It also has a distinct handling problem; when there is moisture in the air, and given enough time, it coagulates into lumps, and sticks to surfaces, which causes handling problems during evacuation of the bins. Additionally, the end user requires screening and milling of soda ash lumps above 2mm before loading into Jumbo bags.
Our office designed, supplied, and installed a complete system of 25 tph Soda Ash crushing and screening, weighing, and jumbo bag loading station. The system also has an additional dust collection system.
System consists of
Bin feeder
Completely enclosed chain conveyor,10 meters length,25tph
Chain bucket elevator, 14m height, 25 tph
Triple deck vibrating screen, for 2mm, 4mm, and 10mm deck screens, 4m length x 1.5 m width, completely enclosed, with special protection for the 2mm deck screen.
Hammer mill, for breakage of lumps from 150mm feed, at 25 tph, with magnet protection for metal debris.
Jumbo bag filling station, with automatic weighing, and closing of feeding gates when correct measure is reached.
Dust collection system, with reverse pulse jet self cleaning filter.
